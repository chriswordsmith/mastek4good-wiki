## Conclusions

The overarching theme is that we are tackling a very human problem. No two client interactions will ever be the same and, indeed, can vary wildly based on circumstances and the people involved.

Safety of both the outreach workers and the clients themselves is of paramount importance.

Ultimately, trust has to be earned with each client. The use of recording equipment is most likely not going to be possible, as there is often deep mistrust of such things from the client side. It is therefore essential that we make it as easy as possible for case workers to capture what has been said with a client as soon and as accurately as possible.

Building a client record is also not often straightforward. Sometimes there will be little available information, both from clients themselves and from other third party care services. However we capture information will need to handle these things.

Case workers deal with multiple cases on an ongoing basis. Context switching may become an issue where cases become complex. This is also something that needs keeping in mind.

Initial outreach to new clients is most often done in pairs. There are sometimes external factors that need taking into account as well. For example, controlling influences nearby might make getting accurate information from a client difficult. Information may change over time based on how comfortable a client is on any given day.
