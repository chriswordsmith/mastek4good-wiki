# Group 4: Ways of Working

## Purpose

First draft of a charter to explore shared principles, processes, and behaviours that guide how a team collaborates.

## Principles

The project is a community undertaking. Ways of working must allow for, and enable, transient members with variable time and capacity.

This also means that a hierarchical structure creates risk and alternatives should be considered.

The community must establish and maintain alignment mechanisms. Specifically:

* Create a mission statement.
* Find ways to provide visibility of activity across the community.
* Make information available to and accessible by all members of the community.
* Define standards for documentation, code, and similar outputs.
* Establish guardrails.

We should be outcome focused, as a group and as individuals.

We will use the client's language and terminology.

We will aim to deliver value early and often.

Empowerment is critical. We are all able to move things forward.

We will benefit from drawing on the different perspectives of the community. This means skills, experiences, backgrounds, sectors, company sizes, and cultures.

## Processes

### Onboarding

Create an induction briefing so that new starters can get up to speed quickly. This should be updated frequently to reflect progress on the project.

This should be made available to people to read before joining the project.

### Status Updates

Regular, up to date briefings should be created to keep people aligned and aware of progress.

This should be made available for people to read outside of the meet up sessions.

It should also be recapped at the start of sessions.

### Task Definition

Tasks should be defined in such a way that they are small enough for people to complete quickly. This mitigates the risk of work getting stuck with one person and impacting the bigger picture.

### Tooling

Tooling will support and enable processes.

The tooling set needs to be defined, for example Confluence, Jira, Slack, GitHub, and similar tools.

Tooling needs to be publicly available so that all volunteers can access it.

Standards and patterns should be defined to help with consistency.

## Behaviours

* We have collective ownership. Work should be carried out in such a way that others can take it forward.
* We should feel empowered, and help to empower others.
* We will show respect for each other's work, commitments, and needs.
* We will share what we are doing.
* We will take personal accountability.
* We will be honest about what we can do, with ourselves and with the rest of the community. This means:
  * Letting people know if a task cannot be completed.
  * Asking for help if it is needed.
* We will support each other. If someone asks for help, we will help them.
* People should feel free to stretch themselves. If we want to try something new, we can try it. But this must not be at the expense of the project. We must be honest about our experience and get support if it is needed.
* No one needs to stay in any particular group. We must be aware of the risk of people coming and going, and mitigate against creating points of failure. The project will be made more resilient by people moving around and working in different areas.
* We will follow good engineering practices, such as small units of work, PRs, well-documented software, and unit tests.
