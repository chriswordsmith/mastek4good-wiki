# Problem Statements From Mastrek4Good

## Main Problem Statement

Frontline staff need a case management process that fits the reality of mobile, time-pressured, trauma-informed support work, because the current Inform experience makes it too difficult to capture, access and manage the right information quickly, creating unnecessary admin burden and reducing time spent with the people they support.

## Sub-Problems

### Sub-Problem 1: "In-the-Moment" Capture Is Too Difficult

Frontline staff need to capture key information while they are out supporting people, because the current mobile and on-the-go experience is unreliable and pushes them into workarounds like paper notes, calendars, photos, notebooks and memory.

**How might we help frontline staff capture important information quickly and reliably in the moment, without disrupting the interaction?**

### Sub-Problem 2: Recording a Note Takes Too Many Steps

Frontline staff need a faster way to record straightforward interactions, because adding a note currently involves too many clicks, too many required fields and repeated input even for simple updates.

**How might we reduce the number of steps, fields and decisions needed to record a simple case note or action?**

### Sub-Problem 3: Navigating a Person's Record Is Confusing

Staff need an easier way to find, understand and continue a person's record, because timelines, old notes, tabs and categories are difficult to navigate and do not reflect how the team thinks about cases.

**How might we make it easier for staff to find the right person, understand previous context and continue the record without confusion?**

### Sub-Problem 4: Reporting Needs Are Not Well Balanced With Usability

Staff need to capture the data needed for funding, reporting and accountability, because they do need structured information, but the current design asks for too much in ways that feel unclear, repetitive and poorly prioritised.

**How might we capture the data the organisation needs for reporting while keeping frontline recording simple, relevant and proportionate?**

### Sub-Problem 5: Follow-Up Actions Are Managed Outside the System

Staff need help managing reminders, next steps and follow-up tasks, because the current system does not support this well and workers rely on diaries, calendars and personal memory to avoid missing actions.

**How might we help frontline staff manage reminders, follow-ups and next actions in one place they can trust?**

### Sub-Problem 6: Support Planning and Risk Tools Are Hard to Use

Staff need support planning and risk recording tools that reflect real frontline practice, because the current tools are difficult to use, overly broad and not well aligned with trauma-informed working.

**How might we redesign support planning and risk recording so that it is clearer, more practical and more trauma-informed?**

## Early Indicators of System Requirements

This section translates the research into early requirement signals; these are not final requirements.

They are early indicators of what a better service and system would need to support.

### Functional Requirement Signals

- Ability to create a quick note with minimal mandatory fields for common interactions.
- Reliable mobile-first note capture with confidence that notes have saved.
- Parity between mobile and desktop for key tasks such as viewing details, updating addresses where appropriate, and adding notes.
- Simpler creation and continuation of timelines or equivalent case activity records.
- Easier access to previous context, including clearer visibility of old notes.
- Configurable or simplified activity categories and tick boxes that better reflect frontline work.
- Integrated reminders, to-do items or follow-up actions linked to a person's case.
- Support for non-caseload and caseload users without confusing branching or duplication.
- Improved handling of support plans, risk assessments and serious incident reporting.
- Easier upload or association of documents and forms within the flow of work.
- Safer and easier data capture from related channels such as email, calendar or approved messaging routes.

### Usability and Workflow Requirement Signals

- Reduce clicks, screens and repeated entry for the most common frontline tasks.
- Make the primary path for note entry obvious and consistent.
- Design for interrupted, mobile, low-attention use in real-world environments.
- Make the current status, next actions and follow-up commitments visible at a glance.
- Support quick scan of a person's recent history before or during contact.
- Use language and structures that align with how outreach staff understand the work.
- Support trust-building and trauma-informed practice by avoiding unnecessary repetition and poorly framed questions.

### Non-Functional and Technical Quality Signals

- Reliable saving and clear confirmation that information has been saved.
- Performance on mobile devices that frontline workers actually use.
- Resilience for variable connectivity and interrupted sessions where possible.
- Secure handling of sensitive case information across devices and channels.
- Auditability of key actions without forcing unnecessary front-end burden onto users.
- Clear permissions and data visibility rules across different worker roles.
- Reporting outputs that can be generated without making frontline entry excessively burdensome.
